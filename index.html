bioaware robotics



research

My background is broadly focused in the design, sensing and control of mechatronic and robotic systems with a focus on the application of computer vision techniques for robotic perception, state estimation and control. I am particularly interested in applying computer vision and other sensing modalities to biomedical applications to further medical device autonomy and to aid navigation, localization and task-planning capabilities for these systems.

More generally, I have a strong interest in mechatronic design across many applications (from dog toys and gardening to medical devices and unmanned aircraft) and in the application of feedback/control to these systems. In addition, I am interested in developing robust experimental methods for evaluating feedback/control and robotic systems in real-time.

about

I am an experimental roboticist interested in robot perception challenges and mechatronic system design and validation. I completed my undergraduate degree in Mechatronics at North Carolina State and the University of North Carolina at Asheville via their joint degree program in 2013 and I completed my PhD and Masters degrees from the University of Colorado, Boulder in 2015 and 2019 respectively. I am currently working as a postdoctoral researcher at the University of Colorado where my research focuses on the challenge of robotic endoscope perception in vivo and on autonomous navigation and the application of Simultaneous Localization and Mapping (SLAM) algorithms for robotic endoscope systems.

In addition to my love for robotics research, I enjoy running, hiking and woodworking and have been known to practice classical guitar on occasion. I have volunteered as the head fencing coach at the University of Colorado for the past 5 years where I have been fortunate to work with talented young fencers across all experience levels! When I am not under a stay-at-home order, you can find me trying my best to work, while casually sipping coffee at any number of local shops in downtown Boulder.
